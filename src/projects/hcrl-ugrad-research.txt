/section Undergraduate Research: Human-Centered Robotics Lab (HCRL)

/subsection Overview
At the Human-Centered Robotics Lab (HCRL) at UT Austin, I contributed to the development of humanoid and mobile robot platforms through mechanical design, structural validation, and software integration.
My work focused on two primary systems: Draco 3, a humanoid robot, and BumpyBot, an omnidirectional transport robot.

/section Draco 3 (Humanoid Research Platform)

%input draco-3.png

/subsection Objective
Draco 3 is a humanoid robot designed for research into whole-body control and hybrid actuation.
I provided mechanical design improvements and hardware reliability support for PhD-level research, focusing on structural integrity and sensor precision.

/subsection Key Engineering Contributions
/bullet
%input draco-encoder
Encoder Mount Redesign: When the knee motors were upgraded for higher load capacity, I designed a custom encoder mount to resolve mounting incompatibilities. The part was fabricated using black SLA resin to ensure high dimensional accuracy and stiffness while reducing signal noise.
%input draco-ankle
Ankle Force-Torque (F-T) Integration: I replaced unreliable strain gauges with ATI Mini45 F-T sensors and engineered a custom mount optimized for manufacturability. I verified the design using Finite Element Analysis (FEA), achieving a safety factor of 2.0.
%input draco-ankle-2
Kinematic and Impact Improvements: I redesigned ankle stoppers and sensor mounts to correct off-center roll motion and expand ankle range of motion. To mitigate impact forces during locomotion, I prototyped and produced in-house rubber soles.
/endbullet

/subsection Maintenance and Operations
I served as a primary hardware technician for Draco 3, diagnosing mechanical and electrical failures during experimental runs.
I developed standardized procedures for belt retensioning, encoder realignment, and sensor calibration, significantly reducing system downtime.

/section BumpyBot (Omnidirectional Mobile Robot)

%input bumpy-bot

/subsection Objective
BumpyBot is an omnidirectional mobile platform designed for autonomous hospital transport research.
My role involved bridging the gap between hardware and autonomy through control integration and navigation software.

/subsection Software and Control Integration
/bullet

Teleoperation: I implemented an Xbox controller interface in ROS, providing an intuitive manual override system for safe testing and data collection.
Signal Processing: I developed and integrated a first-order low-pass filter into the velocity control stack. This smoothed wheel commands and reduced jerk, resulting in more stable and predictable motion during high-speed maneuvers.
Sensor Fusion and SLAM: I rebuilt the URDF model to ensure tf transform accuracy, and integrated odometry plus SLAM to enable autonomous indoor mapping and navigation.
/endbullet

/subsection Summary
My contributions to BumpyBot spanned the full robotics stack, from low-level motor filtering and URDF modeling to high-level SLAM and autonomous navigation, moving the platform closer to real-world hospital deployment.
